This document shows real outputs from the same prompt, comparing what the AI produces with and without the skill context.

## Test Case: Introvert Dating App

### Prompt Given

> I'm building a dating app specifically designed for introverts. Target users are people aged 25-40 who find traditional dating apps overwhelming. The core problem is that introverts struggle with the high-volume, superficial matching that apps like Tinder promote - they want deeper connections without the exhaustion.
>
> Please help me with product discovery for this idea.

## WITH Skill (Score: 4.85)

### What You Get: Expert-Level Discovery Brief

```
# Product Discovery: Introvert Dating App

## Problem Statement
Introverts aged 25-40 experience matching fatigue and superficial 
connections when using traditional dating apps, seeking meaningful 
romantic relationships without the overwhelming volume and pressure.

## Jobs to Be Done Analysis

### The Struggling Moment (First Thought)
Trigger Events:
- Opens Tinder/Bumble, feels immediately drained by endless profiles
- Goes on yet another first date that feels like a job interview
- Deletes dating app after feeling burned out, then re-downloads 
  weeks later out of loneliness

### Push Forces (Why they'd leave current solution)
- Swipe fatigue: "I can't process 50 profiles in 10 minutes anymore"
- Surface-level matching: Conversations die after "hey, how's your weekend?"
- Performance pressure: Feeling like they need to be "on" immediately

### Pull Forces (What attracts them to new solution)
- Quality over quantity: Fewer, more thoughtful matches
- Reduced pressure: No need to be immediately charming
- Meaningful conversations: Built-in prompts for substantial dialogue

### Resistance Forces (What blocks change)
- Anxiety: "What if there are even fewer people on a niche app?"
- Habit: "At least I know how Tinder works, even if I hate it"
- FOMO: "What if I miss someone great on the mainstream apps?"

### Emotional Job
- Desired feeling: Calm confidence, authentic connection, understood
- Current feeling: Drained, performative, like they're bad at dating

## Target User Profile

### Ideal Early Adopter: "The Thoughtful Professional"
- Age 28-35, career-focused but lonely
- Has tried mainstream apps, gets matches but relationships fizzle
- Values depth over breadth in all relationships

### Who This is NOT For (Yet)
- Casual daters looking for hookups
- People who enjoy the gamification of mainstream apps
- Extreme introverts who avoid all online interaction

## Validation Plan

### Level 1: Problem Validation (This Week)
- Method: Cold outreach to r/introvert, MBTI groups, LinkedIn
- Message: "I'm researching how introverts experience online dating"
- Target: 25 people who mention dating app fatigue
- Success: 40%+ respond + willingness to 15-min call

### Level 2: Demand Test (If Level 1 passes)
- Method: Landing page with waitlist
- Headline: "Dating that doesn't drain you"
- Success: 15%+ visitor-to-signup rate

### Level 3: Value Delivery (If Level 2 passes)
- Method: Concierge matchmaking service
- Success: 40%+ would be "very disappointed" to lose it

## PMF Success Criteria

We have PMF when:
- 40%+ of weekly active users would be "very disappointed" without app
- Average 3+ substantial messages exchanged before first date
- Users open app 2-3x per week (vs. binge-delete cycles)
- Date-to-second-date rate >50%

## Immediate Next Steps

1. This week: Post in r/introvert, r/dating, INFJ/INFP groups
2. Interview 5-10 people about their dating app deletion stories
3. Map emotional journey: download → burnout → deletion → re-download
```
